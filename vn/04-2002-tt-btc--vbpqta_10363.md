# Thông tư 04/2002/TT-BTC

CIRCULAR No. 04/2002/TT-BTC of January 16, 2002 guiding the use of State budget development investment capital for the procurement of assets, supplies, equipment and facilities for the construction of working offices
Administrative and public-service agencies and units; political organizations, socio-political organizations, social organizations, socio-professional organizations, and State enterprises, when using capital construction investment capital allocated from the State budget to procure assets, supplies, and equipment for investment projects on building working offices, shall have to buy home-made goods.
Home-made goods are those which are produced, processed or assembled in the Vietnamese territory by organizations and individuals of all economic sectors prescribed in the Planning and Investment Minister�s Decision No. 214/1999/QD-BKH of April 26, 1999 promulgating the list of machinery, equipment and special-use transport means which can be produced at home to serve as basis for the implementation of the Law on Value Added Tax; Decision No. 230/2000/QD-BKH of May 4, 2000 issuing the list of raw materials, materials and semi-finished products which can be produced at home; Decision No. 462/2000/QD-BKH of September 15, 2000 and Decision No. 227/2001/QD-BKH of May 17, 2001 issuing the list of constructional supplies which can be produced at home; and other documents on the lists of home-made materials, equipment and facilities.
This Circular shall not apply to the procurement of assets, supplies and equipment for the following projects on building working offices:
- Investment projects of Vietnamese representations in foreign countries;
- Cases of procurement of imported assets, supplies, equipment and facilities of ODA projects as prescribed in agreements or treaties signed with foreign parties;
- Investment projects using supplies and equipment which cannot be produced at home;
- Investment projects not using capital construction investment capital allocated from the State budget.
This Circular takes effect as from October 1, 2001.-
